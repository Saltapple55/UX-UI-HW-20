WEB RE-DESIGN: Chess’n Math Association

Project Members:

Audrey Tjandra, Maaza Ali, Ishita Sawant

Project Overview

Chess’n Math Association is a non-profit organization dedicated to teaching children chess. They run extracurriculars in various schools and cities at an affordable price, fostering the development of the brain.

However, Chess’n Math had some difficulty getting new customers with their website, especially with competitors arising. We set to re-design the website to make it more appealing to customers

Our Responsibilities

We first did research on current and potential customers to find out their issues with the website. Taking those issues, we created a user persona to base the redesign for. All of then contributed to creating and iterating a wireframe, taking redesign elements from answers to create our current prototype.

In particular, I was the representative from Chess’n Math Association, and was thus able to speak for the company’s needs to my team. My position allowed me to reach out to current customers more easily. My insight also informed a lot of redesign decisions for the initial wireframes, what was needed and what was redundant.

Research & Findings

Research

5 User Interviews

15-20 Minutes each, asking for their motivations for lessons, what they were looking for, and their opinions on the website

1 Stakeholder Interview

Learning what he expected from the re-design, including things he disliked, and what he wanted out of the re-design

Survey

A survey to the parents of existing customers. They were asked about how they felt about using the website, and in particular, getting data about their booking process and asking for what they, as regulars, thought could be improved.

Findings

Cluttered Homepage

The many banners on the home page are disorganized, leading customers to become confused.

Long Checkout

The checkout process is very long and time-consuming because there is a lot of information to put each time, and the company does not save customer information.

Difficult Navigation

Finding the registration page for programs was difficult because the website was crowded, and had too many pages.

USER INSIGHT

Users get deterred from using Chess’n Math due to the confusing navigation and the many steps it takes to book a class online.

PROBLEM SOLUTION

We believe, that by redesigning the Chess’n Math website with a focus on creating an organized and seamless navigation with emphasis on a simple design, will reduce the time it takes for a user to engage with the website by half and increase the number of registrations.

We will know our redesign is successful if we manage to address the design and navigation challenges and thus increase the number of registrations and website traffic

Low-Fidelity Prototype

Based on initial feedback from interviews, we focused on simplifying the registration process by allowing customers to filter classes based on time and skill preferences.

In addition, the number of pages for checkout have been reduced, with the option to bypass the process through login.
